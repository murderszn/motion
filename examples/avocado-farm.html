our story

Three generations of sun & soil

My grandfather planted the first Reed avocado tree in 1962. My mother dry-farmed Hass in clay-rich soil that nobody else wanted. I took over in 2019 and committed to one thing: growing the best avocado you've ever tasted, without a single shortcut.

We don't irrigate. We don't spray. We let the trees fight for water deep in the earth, which concentrates the oils and flavor. Every fruit is hand-picked, sorted by oil density, and packed the same day it leaves the branch.

Summer 2026 is our biggest harvest yet. Pre-order now and be first in line when the crates arrive in July.

from our kitchen

Recipes worth the wait

When your avocados arrive perfectly ripe, these are the first three things we make. Simple, fast, and built to let the fruit speak for itself.

10 min · Serves 2

Perfect Guacamole

Two ripe Hass, flaky sea salt, lime juice, and a pinch of cumin. Mash by hand — never a blender. Finish with diced red onion and cilantro from the garden.

5 min · Serves 1

Grove Gold Toast

Thick sourdough, smashed Reed avocado, chili flakes, and a drizzle of our cold-press oil. The Reed's round shape makes the perfect dome on every slice.

15 min · Serves 4

Citrus Avocado Salad

Fuerte slices, blood orange segments, arugula, and pistachios. The Fuerte's herbal notes pair beautifully with bright citrus and a light vinaigrette.

how we grow

Growing without shortcuts

We dry-farm on clay-rich hillsides that most growers abandoned decades ago. The trees work harder, the fruit tastes better, and the land stays healthier.

💧
Dry-Farmed

No irrigation. Trees send roots 40 feet deep into mineral-rich clay, concentrating oils and flavor in every fruit.

🐝
Pollinator-Friendly

Wildflower corridors between rows attract native bees. We never spray — the ecosystem does the pest control.

♻️
Zero Waste

Overripe fruit becomes cold-press oil. Skins and pits feed our compost. Wooden crates are returnable and reusable.

🌡️
Oil-Density Sorting

Every avocado is tested for oil content before packing. Only fruit above 20% oil makes it into a Grove Box.
